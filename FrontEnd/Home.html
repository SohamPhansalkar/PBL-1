Seismic Activity & Human Impact

Geological research indicates that while most earthquakes are natural, human activities—such as fracking, large-scale mining, and reservoir-induced seismicity—can significantly destabilize the Earth's crust. By injecting high-pressure fluids or removing massive amounts of rock, we inadvertently lubricate fault lines or alter stress distributions.
